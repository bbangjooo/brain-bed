## 5. Dev.to Article

**Title**: Brain Fry is real. Here's what I built to deal with it.

```markdown
## The feeling you know but don't talk about

You've been in Claude Code for hours. You're approving tool calls
without reading them. You know you should stop. You don't. Because
the next answer is right there. Just one more prompt.

Then you stand up and it hits you. Your head is foggy. Your eyes
burn. You can't remember what you were working on 30 minutes ago.
You're not tired — you're fried. Specifically, mentally fried in
that way that only happens after hours of continuous AI interaction.

We call it Brain Fry. Everyone who uses AI coding tools intensely
knows exactly what it feels like. We joke about it in every dev
community. "I'm cooked." "Send help." "My brain is soup."

But nobody does anything about it. Because the next prompt is
always 30 seconds away.

## Why AI coding is different

Traditional coding has natural pauses. You think, you type, you
get stuck, you walk away, you come back. The rhythm forces rest.

AI coding has no rhythm. The AI responds instantly, so there's
never a reason to stop. You make judgment call after judgment call
with zero breathing room. Read the response. Decide if it's right.
Figure out what to ask next. Repeat. For hours.

Your brain doesn't get the micro-breaks it needs to consolidate
and recover. The result is a kind of cognitive overload that feels
like productivity but is actually degradation.

The tell? Your prompts get worse. Shorter. Vaguer. More "just do
it" and less actual thinking. You start re-asking things. You
approve changes without understanding them. You feel busy but
you're not really there anymore.

## What I built

I couldn't make myself take breaks. Timers didn't work — I'd
dismiss them. Pomodoro didn't work — I'd cheat. I needed something
that physically prevents me from continuing.

Brain Bed is a macOS menu bar app that reads your AI CLI session
files locally (Claude Code, Gemini CLI, Codex CLI) and measures
how fried you're getting. It calculates a Brain Fry Index — a
score that reflects your interaction intensity, not just how long
you've been sitting.

When the score gets dangerous, it forces a meditation break:
- Your keyboard locks. You can't type.
- Classical music fades in. Debussy. Satie. Chopin.
- A breathing circle appears. In... hold... out...
- Quotes rotate gently.

You can't code. You can only sit there and realize how tired
you actually were.

## The thing that surprised me

After a 10-minute forced break, my first prompt is better than
my last 50 before the break. Not a little better — noticeably,
obviously better. Clearer thinking. Better structure. Actually
reading the responses instead of skimming.

The break doesn't cost productivity. Skipping it does.

## Try it

Brain Bed is free and open source.

https://brainbed.backproach.dev

If you use AI coding tools for more than a couple hours a day,
your brain is working harder than you realize.
Give it a rest.
```
