Features

How Brain Bed Prevents AI Brain Fry

Brain Bed sits in your macOS menu bar, monitoring your Claude Code, Gemini CLI, and Codex CLI sessions. When your cognitive load gets too high, it steps in.

Brain Fry Index

A real-time 0–100 cognitive load score. Calculated from your message velocity, number of active AI sessions, time of day, and how many alerts you've dismissed. Tracks Claude Code, Gemini CLI, and Codex CLI simultaneously.

Forced Meditation

When your BFI spikes, Brain Bed locks your keyboard and transforms your screen into a meditation space. A breathing circle guides you through 4-4-6-2 cycles while curated classical music — Satie, Debussy, Chopin — fades in. Inspirational quotes rotate gently. You can't work. You can only rest.

Smart Notifications

Context-aware alerts that explain why you should rest. Late-night coding? Rapid-fire prompting? Multiple sessions at once? Brain Bed tells you exactly what's pushing your cognitive load up — and only nudges you when it actually matters (Heating and Brain Fry stages).

AI Brain Fry

What is AI Brain Fry?

AI Brain Fry is the state of cognitive overload that developers experience after prolonged, intense interaction with AI coding tools like Claude Code, Gemini CLI, and Codex CLI. Unlike traditional coding fatigue, AI brain fry hits faster and harder — because AI tools accelerate your output beyond what your brain can sustainably process.

When you're rapid-firing prompts, reviewing AI-generated code across multiple sessions, and context-switching between projects at machine speed, your cognitive load skyrockets. Your brain is doing more reviewing, deciding, and integrating than ever before — but it still needs the same rest it always did.

⚡ Prompt fatigue
You keep rephrasing the same question, unable to articulate what you actually need.
🧊 Decision paralysis
You accept AI suggestions without reviewing them, or reject everything on instinct.
🌀 Context blur
You forget which project you're working on or what you were trying to accomplish 10 minutes ago.
🔥 Compulsive prompting
You keep starting new AI sessions even though you know you should stop.

AI brain fry isn't a character flaw — it's a natural consequence of working at the human-AI boundary. The solution isn't to use AI less, but to take forced breaks before your brain hits its limit. That's exactly what Brain Bed does: it monitors your real-time cognitive load and intervenes with meditation when you need it most.
